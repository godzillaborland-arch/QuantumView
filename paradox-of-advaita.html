PARADOX OF ADVAITA
Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad 4.3.20 (Bhāṣya)
Knowledge increases. Avidyā decreases.
But even a hair-breadth of "I am not all" is still AVIDYĀ.

ŚAṄKARA SAYS
Even a knower may, at times, experience the vision of oneness (sarvātma-bhāva), just as in dream one becomes a king. But as long as even a hair-breadth of 'I am not all' remains, ignorance remains, and return continues.
— Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad 4.3.20 (Bhāṣya)
THE CRITERION
Not the amount of knowledge, but the absence of ignorance determines liberation.
When even the tiniest notion of 'otherness' is gone, only then is there no return.
Knowledge can rise to the highest.
But until 'I am not all' is completely gone, there is still avidyā.
Zero ignorance alone is true liberation.
