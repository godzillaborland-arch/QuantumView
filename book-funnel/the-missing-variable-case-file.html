The Missing Variable

There are two ways to move a mind toward stillness. One is a shepherd driving sheep into a pen — force, vigilance, and the instant you look away they scatter. That's discipline alone: the executive doing everything right while the sheep keep escaping. The other is a bird returning to its nest at dusk — not driven but pulled, drawn home by something it already belongs to. The Yogic tradition calls that pull Pratyak Pravahata, natural inwardness; as Swami Bhajanananda put it, trying to drive the mind inward like a shepherd is not meditation. Under a tree in Chennai in 1978, beside a Royal Enfield, that pull got switched on — through transmission, not instruction — and it never faded. The narrator didn't earn the nest. It was given. That is the missing variable.
