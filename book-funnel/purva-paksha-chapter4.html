A friend's skepticism. Four sharpened challenges to the Tesla = Dhyāna analogy. The tradition has a name for taking an objection seriously before answering it — purva-paksha, the prior position, stated in its strongest form.

Four objections. One friend. A real question.

After the Tesla-Dhyāna analogy came together, the author shared it with a close friend — a serious Vedanta practitioner who wasn't persuaded. Her skepticism opened a door, and behind it were four sharper objections. Each deserves to be honored in its strongest form before being answered. Read each objection carefully, then choose the response that actually engages it — not the one that dismisses it, redefines it, or quietly changes the subject.
