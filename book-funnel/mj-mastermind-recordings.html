Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Retail Economics & The B&N 300 Rule

Amazon takes roughly 65% discount, Barnes & Noble ~50%, and indie stores ~40%. Hitting 300 pre-orders on B&N is the key threshold that influences their decision to physically stock your book in stores.

Retail Phrasing

Always push brick-and-mortar publicly. Tell your audience the book is “available wherever books are sold” and say “international bestseller” rather than “Amazon bestseller.” Never direct bulk sales to Amazon—they may ship 200 books in 200 individual boxes.

Bookmarks & ARCs

Print bookmarks with a book thumbnail, short description, and QR code pointing to your pre-order site (limit ~250 for events). Distribute Advance Reader Copies with special stickers to make early readers feel special and build launch momentum.

The “Bloom Crew” Strategy

Build an early waitlist and convert them into a launch crew. Give them a shareable digital kit and ask them shamelessly to request your book at regional libraries.

AI Copyright Warning

You can use Grok or ChatGPT for brainstorming and sentence clarity, but AI-generated content cannot be legally copyrighted. Your book must remain distinctly written by you.

The “Five Things” Rule

Do not get overwhelmed trying every marketing tactic at once. Consistently execute just three to five marketing actions a week—that alone puts you ahead of the majority of authors.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

The “Two Bridges” of Marketing

Build the Authority Bridge by sharing your publishing journey the moment you decide to write. Once you’ve invested the audience early, crossing the Permissions Bridge (asking them to pre-order) happens automatically.

Retail Placements & Ebook Promos

Drop your ebook price to $0.99, $1.99, or $2.99 during publication week to drive high-volume sales and accidentally hit bestseller lists.

Hudson’s Airport Placement

You can pay ~$5,000 for Hudson’s Airport physical cover-out placement, or simply leverage their free website listing for brand credibility at no cost.

The Blank Book Photo Hack

If your physical book isn’t printed yet, hold a blank white book in your photos. Use AI or Photoshop to overlay your cover onto it afterward for polished marketing materials.

LinkedIn Algorithm Hack

Simply “liking” fellow authors’ posts does not boost the algorithm. You must write a comment on their LinkedIn posts to trigger the algorithmic push.

Avoid Download Codes

One-time-use digital download codes are clunky. It’s far more efficient to set up an autoresponder that sends the direct landing page link to your readers automatically.

Maximize Cover Reveals

A cover reveal with a countdown generates massive early traffic and can even attract potential sponsors for your launch.

Sell Without Selling

At workshops, weave your book into a story and attach a free coaching session for anyone who pre-orders on the spot.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Amazon Review Milestones

25 reviews unlocks better co-op advertising. 50 reviews unlocks A+ content. At 100 reviews, Amazon begins spending their own money on keyword advertising for your book.

The Endorsement Hack

Pre-write 6–12 sample endorsements and let the endorser simply pick a number or tweak it slightly. This dramatically increases your success rate by removing the effort barrier.

Hiring a Publicist

Hire a publicist only as a “designated gorilla” with strict boundaries. Without a backend monetization funnel (e.g., a $197 product), you will likely spend more than you earn in royalties.

PodMatch

Use mypodmatch.com to find and book targeted podcast shows directly—rather than paying a publicist to handle outreach.

Physical vs. Digital Perceived Value

Physical books carry significantly more perceived value and authority in funnels than ebooks. Weight matters.

Tax Compliance at Live Events

Check with your local CPA regarding your state’s specific laws for collecting sales tax when selling author copies at live events.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

The Book Signing “Crowd” Illusion

Never sit or stand behind your signing table—always stand out in front of it to engage people. Bring at least three people who love you to the event and keep two of them standing in line talking to you at all times to create an immediate illusion of high demand.

The “Wandering Shill” Bookstore Tactic

Task a third friend or family member to wander the store carrying an autographed copy of your book. Have them actively talk you up to other shoppers (“Oh my gosh, I can’t believe this author is here today—you have to go check them out!”) to physically herd traffic over to your line.

Inscription Efficiency Rule

Do not try to write custom, ad-libbed messages on the fly at a busy signing—you will inevitably start writing down the words you are speaking and ruin the book. Memorize a fixed, standard phrase in advance. Write the reader’s name, insert your standard phrase, sign it, and explicitly date it. Have readers write their names on a 3×5 notepad first to avoid spelling errors.

The Retail “Trunk Trick”

Bookstores under-order event stock constantly. Never go to a signing without a backup case of books hidden in your vehicle trunk. If the store sells out, tell the manager you can supply your own books—they will facilitate the sales on-site and reimburse you by ordering replacement stock from the publisher later.

The 3-Visit Retail Relationship Strategy

Do not walk into a bookstore cold and pitch a signing—you will be met with an automatic “no.” Instead, use a three-step pipeline:

Visit 1: Browse the store, buy something small, make friends with a staff member (ideally the Community Relations Manager), and leave.
Visit 2: Return a week or two later while the connection is warm, repeat the friendly interaction, buy something small, and leave.
Visit 3: Explain that you are a locally distributed author and pitch the signing as a collaborative event where you are bringing your entire network into their store to buy products.

Media-Driven Signing Bookings

Proactively pitch local print, radio, and TV stations 60 days before a planned event. Secure a short media interview snippet, then leverage that media exposure to prove to the bookstore manager you are bringing guaranteed traffic.

Leveraging Regional Store Managers

Once you execute a successful signing at one location, call the store manager in your next target market. Name-drop the manager you just worked with to establish instant internal retail credibility.

Post-Launch Textual Error Corrections

The publisher retains massive flexibility for interior text updates post-launch. Submit a support ticket and the publisher will update the digital master files so all future printings are completely corrected.

Custom Audiobook Strategy

Audiobooks do not have to be recorded word-for-word if standard prose reading doesn’t fit your format. You can record an abbreviated custom audio walkthrough guiding listeners through the concepts, paired with downloadable companion worksheets or PDFs.

Strategic Audio Delay

Release your audiobook 3 months after your print publication date. This keeps early audio sales from cannibalizing the initial print pre-orders needed for physical bookstore shelf placement, while giving you a powerful secondary “launch week” milestone to promote later.

Pre-Pub Bulk Sales & Bestseller Stacking

If an organization or business wants to buy multiple copies before your official publication date, coordinate the purchase through Beantown Books. They will hold the physical reporting data and stack it directly into your launch week numbers to maximize your shot at hitting major bestseller lists.

April 21, 2026

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Audiobook Production

Always record in your own voice to build listener connection. If impossible, use Futuri Books—a human-managed AI voice clone service for around $2,200.

PodMatch Proactivity

Do not wait for hosts to reach out. Proactively message them and tweak your profile at least once every three months. Repurpose podcast interviews into short video clips for Instagram to prove you are a good guest.

Chamber of Commerce Limo Tour

Pitch local Chamber of Commerce branches for speaking gigs, or rent a limo and do a tour across different cities for maximum exposure.

The Self-Shipping Hack

Buy a roll of 3M Scotch bubble wrap packaging instead of envelopes and tape. The material sticks to itself, so you wrap a book, trim, slap on a label, and ship instantly.

Bookstore Signings

Never show up without a backup case of your own books—stores frequently sell out or order late. Request a one-time consignment deal if necessary.

Amazon “Browsing” Algorithm

Amazon categorizes your book based on what the user was browsing before they clicked your link. Request a global metadata refresh if you end up falsely ranked in an unrelated category.

Instant Bestseller Verification

If you rank under 100 in any Amazon category, you are officially an Amazon Bestseller. Take a screenshot immediately—Amazon will not notify you.

Frankfurt Book Fair Warning

Skip offers (e.g., $2,000) to feature your book at Frankfurt. It is a rights event for publishers, not a consumer fair—expect zero consumer sales.

The “Free Content” Hack

Never offer a “Free Chapter”—readers assume you’ll pressure them to buy. Call it “Free Content” or a “Free Report” instead and capture their email with less resistance.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Promotional Platforms Compared

NetGalley is best for early reviews, influencer buzz, and library awareness. BookBub is a massive sales engine ideal for discounted ebooks ($0.99). Book Movement is niche and meant for book clubs.

NetGalley Ecosystem Warning

Do not simply upload your book to NetGalley and walk away. Without a larger outreach campaign, it’s like “putting flyers on a windshield in a rainstorm.”

Hudson’s Placement Specifics

A negotiated placement exists for ~$4,800: 50+ stores for 30 days, cover-out placement, with guaranteed overnight replenishment.

MJA Discount Code Nuance

Discount codes on morganjamesbooks.com range from MJA5 to MJA40 (max 40% off). The code MJA100 does not work.

The Auto-Responder Hack

To get the most current links and forms without waiting for a reply, send a blank email to authorsupport@morganjamespublishing.com—an autoresponder immediately replies with FAQs and critical links.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Pitching Bulk Sales & Sponsors

Pitch event organizers by framing the conversation entirely around what’s in it for their audience. Alternatively, pitch the sponsors of the event to buy books to give away at their booths.

B&N Pre-Order Bribes

B&N does not accept discount codes for pre-orders. Manually verify pre-orders (e.g., have them email receipts) and use your MJ landing page to deliver an ebook bribe.

Hudson’s Faith Update

Hudson’s Airport stores recently reversed their policy and are now accepting faith-based books in-store, provided there are no violent images (guns/bombs) on the cover.

The ISBN Search Trick

Before your book cover officially populates on retail sites, locate your hidden pre-order links by dropping your 13-digit ISBN directly into Google or bookstore search engines.

Podcast Weaving

Naturally weave your book’s lessons into podcast interviews and speeches. By the time you mention the title at the end, the audience is already anticipating it—removing the clunky sales pitch feeling.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Bestseller List Mechanics

USA Today requires sales across at least 3 different sources (Amazon, B&N, Indies), but weighs indie stores significantly heavier. Pre-pub week is the easiest time to hit it.

Barnes & Noble Bestseller

You must manually scroll the top 100 category lists to find your book and screenshot it—B&N does not display category ranks prominently or notify you.

The Blacklist Warning

Barnes & Noble actively blacklists authors (sometimes for up to 5 years) for promoting Amazon too heavily on public platforms. Never highlight Amazon over B&N in your marketing.

The “Memorized Tagline” Signing Hack

Come up with a short, personalized tagline to write in every book you sign. This lets you write smoothly while talking to readers, preventing you from accidentally inscribing their conversation.

Book Signing Mechanics

Use blue ink to make the signature pop. Keep backup pens in a baggie. Use a fishbowl or iPad form to capture emails. Have readers write their name on a notepad first to avoid misspelling unique names.

Interior Typo Fixes

Post-publication typos or textual errors can be fixed—submit a ticket and the publisher will update the file for future printings and append the copyright date to indicate the corrected version.

Royalty Delay

Royalty payments have a 9-month delay to account for bookstore returns, which average under 15%.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Amazon Review Targets

Target a minimum of 25 reviews as quickly as possible to signal to Amazon that the product is generating buzz.

The Early Ebook Review Hack

You cannot leave an Amazon review until a product is officially for sale. Release the ebook version early (heavily discounted) to allow advanced readers to populate your Amazon page with verified reviews before the physical launch.

Interior Typo Fixes (Pre-Print)

If a severe typo is discovered right before print, email Andrea or submit a ticket and the publisher will quickly fix the interior file.

The ClickBank Backend (John & Jim’s System)

Stop relying solely on royalties. Take a single chapter, transform it into a digital PDF ($17–$27), and launch it on ClickBank to leverage their massive affiliate network. Use upsells (courses, supplements) to push average cart value over $100.

The “Buyers List” Metric

99% of backend income comes from an email list. A list of 10,000 buyers (people who spent $17 on your ClickBank offer) is exponentially more powerful than a free prospect list of 1,000,000—because buyers have proven willingness to spend.

Asset Generation with NotebookLM

Use Google’s NotebookLM to effortlessly generate marketing assets, mind maps, and interactive audio podcasts directly from your manuscript.

Event Sales via Stripe

If doing an early book signing with ARCs, allow buyers to purchase immediately via your own Stripe account or Venmo—capturing the sale right there on the spot.

Marketing Tips & Tactical Hidden Gems

Social Media Breakdown

TikTok/BookTok is ideal for fiction and storytelling-heavy memoirs. To succeed, content must be raw, unpolished, and non-AI generated—posted 3–4 times a day. LinkedIn Reels are best for business authors to establish industry expertise.

Review Contests

Submit to Publisher’s Weekly book review competitions ($30 entry fee). Even if you don’t win, you receive heavyweight professional reviews from the judges that you can use in your marketing.

Powell’s Books Funnel

Strategically funnel 6–10 pre-orders directly to Powell’s Books (the world’s largest indie store) to prompt them to physically stock the book on their shelves.

Publication Is Not the Finish Line

A $7,000 coaching package is far easier to sell than 7,000 individual books. You must build an ecosystem around the book—do not treat publication as the end of the race.

The 5–7 Day Email Course Lead Magnet

Extract the 5–7 most critical points from your book and turn them into an automated email sequence. Place this lead magnet in your book’s front/back matter. By day 7, the reader is perfectly pre-framed to book a high-ticket discovery call.
